My Instagram account has been leaked. What should I do now?

11:00

I'm sorry to hear that your Instagram account has been leaked. Here are some steps you can take to try to regain control and secure your account:

1. Change Your Password Immediately: If you can still access your account, change your password right away. Make sure to choose a strong password that includes a combination of letters, numbers, and symbols.

2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): This adds an extra layer of security to your account. Even if someone knows your password, they won't be able to log in without the second factor, usually a code sent to your phone.

3. Revoke Access to Suspicious Third-Party Apps: Go to your Instagram settings and review the third-party apps that have access to your account. Revoke access to any apps that you don't recognize or trust.

4. Report the Issue to Instagram: If you can't access your account or if you suspect that it has been compromised, report the issue to Instagram immediately. They may be able to help you recover your account.

5. Check for any Unauthorized Activity: Look through your account for any posts, messages, or changes that you didn't make. This can give you an idea of what the person who accessed your account did.

6. Notify Your Followers: Once you've regained control of your account, consider posting a message to let your followers know that your account was compromised. This can help prevent them from falling victim to any scams or malicious activity.

7. Monitor Your Account: Keep an eye on your account for any further suspicious activity. If you notice anything unusual, take action immediately.

8. Stay Vigilant: Going forward, be cautious about the information you share online and the links you click on. Regularly update your passwords and enable security features like 2FA whenever possible.

If you've done all of these steps and are still having trouble, you may need to reach out to Instagram's support team for further assistance.
